Supporting Your First Step to Studying in Japan

We provide comprehensive support for high school students, university students, and working professionals who wish to study in Japan.

Studying abroad is filled with both anxiety and hope. We strive to reduce that anxiety through our support services.

1. Pre-Study Abroad Consultation

To ensure a regret-free experience, we offer free pre-study abroad consultation services.
Feel free to discuss any concerns, worries, or questions you may have.
We provide information based on facts and real-world examples.

2. Pre-Study Abroad Experience Course

Through individual counseling, we listen to your hopes and goals to propose the best study abroad plan.
We provide advice on selecting study destinations, programs, and budgeting during the initial planning stage.
If you're interested in studying abroad, we recommend visiting Japan beforehand to see it with your own eyes.

Pre-Study Abroad Experience Course Details

  • 1. Visit Japan with your family
  • 2. Tour potential schools in person
  • 3. Experience Japanese housing, meals, and daily life
  • 4. Compare your study abroad goals with reality to make an informed decision

Studying abroad is a significant life experience with lasting impacts, so we recommend making the decision after experiencing Japan with your family.
If it doesn’t align with your goals, it’s okay to reconsider studying in Japan.

3. Study Abroad Application Process

Once your study destination is decided, the next step is to proceed with the application process.
We formally apply to the chosen school or program on your behalf.
This often involves submitting online application forms or mailing documents such as past transcripts, recommendation letters, or essays.
After the application is accepted, interviews or additional documents may be required, so we ensure you stay informed of school communications.
We handle the application process for students.

After receiving formal acceptance, we assist with arrangements for housing and daily life at your study destination.
Many schools offer options like dormitories or homestays, and we help you choose the best accommodation.
We provide comprehensive support for your study abroad life, offering multiple housing plans.

Preparing an International Cash Card

Preparing a credit card or international cash card for use during your study abroad is crucial.
Contracts for services like mobile phones often require a credit card, and an international cash card ensures smooth transactions.
If the student has no income, we recommend parents obtain an international cash card, with the student using a family card.

4. Visa Application

Once you receive approval from your study destination, the next step is to apply for a visa.
Visa types and application processes vary by country, so we guide you to check the latest information on the embassy or consulate website.
Typically, visa applications require a passport, acceptance letter from the school, and proof of financial capability.
After submitting the application, you’ll need to wait for the visa to be issued.

5. Accommodation Arrangements

Travel Preparations

Once the visa is issued, we proceed with travel preparations.
We help book flight tickets based on your travel date and confirm your accommodation details.

Securing Accommodation

Upon arriving in Japan, securing accommodation is the top priority.
It typically takes about one month to complete a rental contract and move in.
For minors, direct contracts are not possible, so we provide support to ensure students’ living arrangements are in place.

6. Japanese Language Learning Support

Japanese Language Learning

The Japanese school year begins in April. We recommend using the period before school starts to study Japanese.
We also offer remote Japanese language learning support before you arrive in Japan.

In addition to Japanese, we can provide academic support, including tutoring by top university students.

7. Cultural Adaptation Support

Adapting to Life in Japan

Understanding Japanese culture not only aids your studies but also helps you grasp mainstream Japanese values, enabling deeper communication.
As part of cultural adaptation, learning Japanese etiquette, daily manners, and language nuances is essential.
Participating in local events or festivals helps you connect with the community and build relationships with locals.

Guidance on Transportation and Shopping

We provide orientation and support for international students, covering local living information, transportation, healthcare access, and administrative procedures.
If you have questions or encounter issues, you can consult your school’s international exchange center or local community groups.

Cross-Cultural Communication

Adapting to a new culture requires flexibility and an open mind.
Misunderstandings due to language barriers or cultural differences may arise, but overcoming them deepens cross-cultural understanding and strengthens relationships.
Actively engaging with locals, learning Japanese, and experiencing Japanese culture and customs will aid your adaptation.
